Industry Capstone, Teledyne FLIR

V.I.P.E.R.

Visual-Infrared Position Estimation for Reconnaissance. A GPS-denied drone navigation payload integrating thermal imaging, visual cameras, and inertial sensors for search-and-rescue operations.

Problem Statement

Most modern drones depend on GPS for flight stability and positioning. In GPS-denied environments like indoor spaces, urban canyons, and dense forests, drones lose reliable navigation. VIPER addresses this by combining visual and infrared imaging with inertial sensing to estimate position without GPS dependency.

My Role: Thermal Simulation and Budget

I led the thermal analysis for the payload, using COMSOL Multiphysics to model three-dimensional conjugate heat transfer under operational flight conditions. My simulations guided the heat sink geometry and material selection, reducing heat sink mass by 30% while maintaining thermal performance. I also managed the project budget, handling all purchasing, vendor coordination, and expenditure tracking across five subteams.

Design and Results

The payload integrates a FLIR Boson thermal camera, a global-shutter visible camera, dual IMUs, an altimeter, and a compass, all driven by a Raspberry Pi CM5 on custom 4-layer PCBs. The aluminum 6063-T6 heat sink with optimized sheet fins keeps all components within safe operating temperatures under the full 12W thermal load. Simulation results closely tracked experimental bench tests, with the CM5 chip peaking at 77 C (well within spec) and average internal air at 49.7 C.
